Cast & Crew Bios

Grant Chism (Auctioneer)
Grant is happy to make his debut with TMC.  This is his fourth BPF production.  Other BPF productions include A Touch of Spring and The Back Room at Fells Point Corner Theatre and The Picture Walls with the Invisible Theatre Company at Mobtown Theatre.  He played Bono in Fences at the Vagabond and at Laurel Mill Playhouse, Donald in You Can't Take it With You and Rashi in A Talent For Murder at Columbia Community Players as well as many plays with the Arena Players.
Saul Clark-Braverman(Lloyd the Panhandler)
Saul attended the American Academy of Dramatic Arts in 1976. After doing numerous roles in high school and college, Saul landed his big role in New York City, portraying a dead, naked body in the play Compulsion. This vaulted him to get numerous other roles as a bartender in various restaurants in New York City. His local theater credits include work at Morgan State University, Dundalk Community Theater, Liberty Showcase Theater, The Jewish Theater Workshop, Fells point Corner Theatre and The Baltimore Playwrights Festival. Saul can also be seen in various local commercials and some film work. Saul Lives in Westminster with his wife Mickey of 29 years.
Buzz (Joseph) Conway (Trumpet)
Buzz, a trumpet, Flugelhorn and Flute Artist, has been a professional musician for over 40 years. He played with The Admirals (show band) for over 25 years and currently plays improvisational music with Deep Tree Montra and variety music with The Majestics, Totally Confused, Gene Vincent & The Cadillac Cruisers, to name a few.   Buzz has played back up for The Beach Boys, Donny & Marie Osmond and various other named acts and has also played several Presidential Inaugural Balls. He has performed  music scores for several movies.   Buzz enjoys teaching  private trumpet and flute  lessons in his spare time.  Contact Buzz via email, buzzconway924@hotmail.com
Charles W. Danforth III (Lighting Designer)
Charlie returns to TMC for its fourth season ready for more "challenges." Among his favorites are Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat, Sunday in the Park with George, and West Side Story. In theatre, Charlie lives by the phrase "If the world's a stage, I want better lighting."
Babs Dentz (Gwen)
Babs works in the Registrar's office at the College of Notre Dame of Maryland and teaches English and Acting Techniques at the College. She has enjoyed working in community theatre for over forty years. She was most recently seen as Rose in the Theatrical Mining Company's production of The Ape On the Church Steps. Some of her favorite roles include those in 'Night Mother (which she used as her Masters project), Octette Bridge Club, The Little Foxes, Pippin, My Fair Lady, Cat On a Hot Tin Roof, Streetcar Named Desire, You Can't Take It With You, The Man Who Came to Dinner, Medea, Agnes of God, Philadelphia, Here I come, Animal Instincts, Caz, Woman on the Edge, Alice, Where Art Thou Going, School Shooters, and many, many more.
Susan Middaugh (Playwright)
Susan, a member of the Dramatists Guild and the Playwrights Group of Baltimore, has  had seven of her short plays receive a total of 13 productions by community theaters in the U.S. and Canada. This is her second production in the Baltimore Playwrights Festival and the second with director Barry Feinstein. The One Act Play Depot in Canada has published her short play, Such Good Neighbors, and England's Lazy Bee Scripts has published her short play, Table for Two. She is a self-employed business writer and owner who also enjoys hiking and being a grandmother. She wants to thank Barry and all the cast for bringing her characters to life.
Barry Feinstein (Director)
Barry has been directing plays in the BPF nearly every year the festival has been in existence. He is thrilled to be opening the fourth season of TMC with Susan Middaugh's play and hopes to direct many more in the future.
Darla Luke (Set Designer)
In addition to designing the poster for The Theatrical Mining Company's production of Black Widows, and last year's Ape on the Church Steps. Darla has designed sets for Spotlighters' Tennessee Williams short plays Four by Tenn and FPCT's The Marriage of Bette and Boo, The Miser, Bug, The Homecoming, and Off the Map. Darla's studio is located in the Fells Point area of Baltimore. Her paintings can be seen at www.ARTBreak.com (search Darla Luke).
Ann Mainolfi (Vera)
Ann has been directing and producing theater in the Baltimore area for nearing fifty years. She is a well-known actor in the area and is the president of The Vagabond Players. The Theatrical Mining Company is honored to have such a talent in our production this summer.
Crystal Sewell (Stage Manager, Shelly Harrington, Insurance Agent)
Crystal is excited to yet again, be stage-managing a Barry Feinstein production. This is her second BPF stage-managing experience, having been stage manager for "$40 Million Dollars, if you want it" in 2006. She is more than ecstatic to also be IN this production, Shelly is a fun character! Thank You Susan, Barry and The Amazing Cast of "Black Widows" for such an interesting, and always entertaining, experience!
Abby Sharbach (Aerobics Instructor/Morgue attendant/ Process Server)
This is Abby's first Baltimore Playwrights production and her community theater debut. She is grateful for the thought-provoking story that Susan has brought to the stage and Barry's talented, patient direction that has brought the story to life.  She couldn't have asked for a better first experience.
Charlie Sigler (Guitarist)
Charlie has studied with Paul Meyers, Michael Raitzyk, Skip Grasso, Steve Herberman, Mike Moreno, Paul Bollenback and Tim Green. A Towson High School Big Band alum, he was also a member of the 2008-09 Maryland All-State Jazz Ensemble. Charlie has performed at many local clubs and restaurants and teaches guitar students. He frequently collaborates with Rhonda Robinson. In addition, he has performed with locals Michael Raitzyk, Craig Alston, Dave Ballou, Warren Wolf and Greg Thompkins. Charlie has performed at the Litchfield Jazz Festival, Station North Arts Festival, Twins Jazz in DC, and The Eubie Blake Center with the Eubie Blake Legacy Band. He is currently pursuing a degree in jazz performance from William Paterson University in Wayne, New Jersey, studying with Paul Meyers.
Glenn Vitale (John McCardle)
Glenn has studied acting for over 20 years. He has trained in NYC; LA; D.C. and Baltimore. He last performed at the Bay Theatre in Annapolis in the role of Saul Kimmer in Sam Shepard's play, "True West." One of his favorite roles was the Night Clerk in Eugene O'Neill's play, 'Hughie." He has also performed in numerous independent films and television productions. Glenn is most appreciative for the opportunity to bring the role of John McCardle to life.
Mike Ware (Moe Goodman)
Mike is glad to be back with the BPF once again.  You may have seen him in last year's BPF production of "G-Man" in the role of Larry.  He has done several other BPF shows over the past few years as well.  He has enjoyed working with Barry and the wonderful cast/crew, but doesn't plan on making any of them a beneficiary on ANY sort of document.  He'd like to live to do a few more years of theatre! Mike lives in Havre de Grace, MD.
