Gay Deceivers: Bios

Alexander Scally (Nelson)
Alexander is thrilled to be making his Baltimore Playwrights Festival debut in Gay Deceivers. An uprising senior pursuing a BFA in Acting at UMBC, Alexander has been participating in Baltimore theatre for many years and has an interest in theatre education. He holds a Maryland Distinguished Scholar Award for Theatre, and received UMBC's Theatre merit scholarship. Recent productions include: Brian's Dance (Dave), She Stoops To Conquer (Hardcastle), The Threepenny Opera (Tiger Brown), Silence, Cunning, Exile (Acquaintance), The Faulkner Project (Tull/McGowan), Aristophanes' Frogs (Dionysus), The Fit American (Danny), The Cagebirds (Gloom). Film: C is for Cabbage (Ronnie).
Barry Feinstein (director)
Barry has been directing with the Baltimore Playwrights Festival since its beginning with Joan Bonato's "An Arrangement of Words". Other plays have included: "Bloodlife" by Dennis Jordan "Every Sussie and Sal" by Carol Weinberg, "Caz", "Dark Eyes", and "Metal Mother and the Rustproof Factor" all by Kathleen Barber, "Good Night", "Smoke", "Walk Like a Man"and "Ella's Song" all by Jim Cary, "Air Ice" by Joe Dennison, "A Modern Pas De Deux" by Susan Middaugh, "The Blue Eye of Robert Oppenheimer" by Vicki Danos and "Blessed Mothers of War" by Ty DeMartino.
Belinda Panelo (Tolly)
A television and film actress in the Philippines. Belinda worked as an MTV-Asia VJ for 3 years before deciding to move to New York to study acting at the Atlantic Theater Company. She is involved with the local film scene as well. With two films in post production this year and another set to film in the fall. She is a busy Mom of 4 year old son Devon who keeps her grounded and reminds her of the important things in life. Belinda is grateful for the BPF. What a wonderful opportunity for playwrights and actors alike! Thank you.
BJ Frank (Light & Sound Tech)
BJ became involved in theatre when he was cast in Tom Sawyer at Franklin Middle School. He has appeared as an actor in Music Man, Damn Yankees, Hello Dolly, and last year's TMC production of Almost Vermilion, among others. BJ would like to thank the cast & crew of Gay Deceivers for the chance to be part of this production. Most of all, he wants to thank his family for all their love and support.
Charles W. Danforth III (Lighting Designer)
Charlie returns to TMC for its second season ready for more "challenges." Among his favorites are Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat, Sunday in the Park with George, and West Side Story. In theatre Charlie lives by the phrase "If the world's a stage, I want better lighting." Thanks to Barry and Terry for all of their support.
Graham Pilato (Warren)
Graham is an actor recently graduated from an eventful two year stint of teaching high school English in St. Mary's County, Maryland. Graham, before teaching, was a Dramatic Arts major at St. Mary's College of Maryland, where he graduated in 2004, having played roles like Harpagon in Moliere's "The Miser" and Deeley in Harold Pinter's "Old Times". While teaching, he also played Old Ekdal in Ibsen's "The Wild Duck", also at St. Mary's College of Maryland. In September and October, he will be seen at the Randolph Road Theater in Silver Spring as a crewman on the famously seized U.S.S. Pueblo in "Pueblo", a Heritage Theatre production.
Janise Whelan (Rose)
Janise moved to Baltimore at the age of 23, where she immediately jumped on stage in a variety of roles. Her favorites are: Sissy in "Come Back to the Five and Dime, Jimmy Dean, Jimmy Dean", Jenny in "Chapter Two", Madge in "Picnic", and Marjorie in "Extremities". Theatre patrons will remember her last role as Mrs. Fenway in The Spotlighters production of Tennessee Williams' "Summer at the Lake", which was one of four plays produced under the title, "4 by Tenn". Thanks to Barry and Penny for thinking of me for the part of Rose.
Josh Bristol (Rudy)
Joshua Bristol is a student of St. Mary’s College of Maryland where he is an active Actor, Director, and Stage Manager. Recent roles include Demetrius in a Midsummer Nights Dream and Arthur in The Balcony at SMCM. He has also recently directed The Actors Nightmare by Christopher Durang as well and four of Samuel Becketts shorter pieces. In December he will be Directing Suburbia by Eric Bogosian as part of the St. Mary’s College main stage season. He has greatly enjoyed doing this role and is proud to be part of the Gay Deceivers
Kathy McCrory (Mrs. Goodwell, Assistant Director, Builder)
Kathy is glad to be spending the summer once again with the Theatrical Mining Company, doing another show. It is also great to be working with Barry Feinstein on another show. Last summer Kathy directed Almost Vermilion for TMC. This past spring she helped direct Mousetrap with Bob Russell. Kathy would like to thank her husband for being so patient with all the late nights, and lack of dinners at home. Jim is her biggest supporter. To the cast Break a Leg and have Fun!!!
Philip Laubner (Photography)
Philip grew up outside of Boston, MA, playing music, taking pictures and eventually moving into the city to work as a graphic artist. He moved to New Orleans in 2002. After Katrina he worked at an under-staffed full service ad agency; wearing many hats, including graphic designer, staff photographer. Philip moved to Baltimore in August of 2007, and has worked as a freelance graphic artist and photographer. His first Baltimore photo exhibit was at CCBC, featuring photographs shot in New Orleans while he marched in the Rex Parade during Mardi Gras 2007.
PS Lorio (Playwright/Set Design/Builder)
Lorio is a nationally produced playwright/composer who resides in Baltimore. Previous Baltimore productions include: PLPD; a little insurance never hurts and Four Scenes: A Hungarian Trilogy both produced by the Mobtown Players; Ebenezer! a Christmas musical, (book by Bryan Zocher, additional lyrics by L.L. Bennett) twice produced at Spotlighters Theatre; Afterlife at Baltimore Theater Project; and Always the Bridesmaid produced by PussyCat Theatre Co. as part of Baltimore's Pride celebration at Queer Café. In 2003, two plays by Lorio, Missing Phil and Ten Reasons Big Betty is Stuck on the Side of the Road in a Little Pink Dress (written with Dahlia Kaminsky), the latter was produced as part of the Baltimore Playwrights Festival. Lorio has been away from theater since her 2004 production of DICK: The Musical at Spots, she is excited to be back!
Roy Hammond (Reverend Godwin)
Roy has appeared in 65 stage productions over the past 35 years. Some of his local appearances include Bob McKellaway in MARY, MARY, Noah Curry in THE RAINMAKER, Father in A CHRISTMAS STORY, Inigo Jones in "For the Return to Albion," (Baltimore Playwright's Festival) and Elwood P. Dowd in HARVEY. Some of his Los Angeles stage credits include Jimmy in THE GINGERBREAD LADY, Jonathan Brewster in ARSENIC AND OLD LACE, Count Dracula in DRACULA and Geoffrey in STEPPING OUT. Film and television credits include THE YOUNG AND THE RESTLESS, NIGHT ANGEL, THE STEPFORD CHILDREN (with Barbara Eden), DO NOT DISTURB, MORGAN FRENZI, NEXT, PLEASE! along with several rock videos and a multitude of commercials. National touring company: HIAWATHA. Off-Broadway: UN BEL DI (Raft Theatre).
Roy Hammond (Set Design/Builder)
Roy has been designing sets for over twenty-five years. In 1996 and 1998, his set designs for THE COCKTAIL HOUR and ANY WEDNESDAY were nominated in Los Angeles for the A.D.A. Award (L.A.'s equivalence to a Tony Award). In 1995, the Los Angeles Times said about his design for CRITIC'S CHOICE, " There's the exquisitely detailed set - the best I've ever seen …" At The Audrey Herman Spotlighters theatre he has designed the sets for THE NIGHT OF THE IGUANA, VERONICA'S ROOM and co-designed the sets for THE ODD COUPLE, ALL MY SONS, and MARY, MARY. Other sets he has designed in the Baltimore/Washington D.C. area have included YOU NEVER KNOW (Greenbelt Arts Center), DR.COOK'S GARDEN (Greenbelt Arts Center), CHEAPER BY THE DOZEN (LST), and DEATHTRAP (Paragon Theatre Company) which City Paper remarked, "As a set designer, Hammond also goes above and beyond the call of duty with an intricately designed single set …"
