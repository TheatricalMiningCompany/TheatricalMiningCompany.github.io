Rodney Bonds, Dewitt
is pleased to be making his first appearance with Theatrical Mining Company. He was last seen on stage as Harry Hope in FPCT's highly acclaimed production of "The Iceman Cometh". Some of Rodney’s favorite roles include Artie in “House of Blue Leaves” (Vags),  Tevye in “Fiddler on the Rood” (Spartanburg Repertory Theater), and Peter Maxwell in “Love and Anger” (FPCT). Rodney is the Chairman of The Baltimore Playwright’s Festival and incidentally, Father in Law to his Director. Rodney would like to thank his wife Deborah and his son Ian for their love, support and tolerance of his eccentric avocation.
photo by Robin Boyle
Tucker Foltz, Jesse
This is Tucker's first BPF production and debut at Load of Fun. He owes the majority of his experience to Northern Stage, a professional regional theatre in his home state, Vermont. Outside of acting, Tucker attended Earlham College for Anthropology and Sociology and has traveled and studied in India twice. He is an enthusiast of music and hiking. His most recent Baltimore production was the 2012 Fells Point Corner Theatre 10X10.
photo by Robin Boyle
Sarah Eberhardt, Sherri
Sarah graduated from Towson University with a BS in Acting. Her most recent roles include Mia and Josie as part of the ensemble for Glass Mind Theatre's Brainstorm3, and Feste in Twelfth Night with the Greenbelt Art Center. She also pirates aboard the Fearless in the Inner Harbor as Nighthawk throughout the plundering season. She would like to thank everyone involved in Hired Gun for making this such an exciting and rewarding experience. Enjoy the show!
photo by Robin Boyle

Mike Ware, Red
Mike is happy to be appearing in another Mark Scharf play for the BPF.  He continues to be busy in theatre with performances so far this year in The Fantasticks (Henry Albertson), Darwin in Malibu (Charles Darwin) and Cabaret (Herr Schultz) all at various Maryland locations.  He sends his best wishes to the cast/crew of Hired Gun.
photo by Robin Boyle
Mark Scharf, Playwright
Mark's plays have appeared across the USA and internationally; for more information, please visit: Mark's website. He last appeared onstage as Larry Slade in FPCT's production of THE ICEMAN COMETH. Mark is a member of The Dramatists Guild of America, Inc.
photo by Robin Boyle

Stacey Bonds, Director
Stacey has been involved in Baltimore theater for years, both on stage and off. After directing two Baltimore Playwrights Festival readings this spring she jumped at the opportunity to direct one of this season's full-length productions. Past directing credits include Grease and The Fifth of July. Previous stage appearances include Godspell (Gilmer), The Rocky Horror Show (Eddie/Dr. Scott), Psycho Beach Party (Chicklet), and Date with a Stranger (Paula). Stacey was a Regional Semi-Finalist in the Irene Ryan Acting competition for the American College Theatre Festival and took the title for "Actor of the Year" that same year. She would like to say a special thanks to her amazing Stage Manager for keeping her balanced and to Mark Scharf for writing this wonderful play.
photo by Robin Boyle
Elisabeth Jonas, Stage Manager
Elisabeth has greatly enjoyed working on her first BPF production this summer with the premiere of Hired Gun. Previous stage management work in the Baltimore theatre community includes several productions with Iron Crow Theatre Company. Elisabeth is a recent graduate of Towson University with degrees in Business Management and Theatre, and is excited to pursue a career in Theatre Management. Much love and gratitude to her family and to the talented and dedicated cast and crew of Hired Gun.
