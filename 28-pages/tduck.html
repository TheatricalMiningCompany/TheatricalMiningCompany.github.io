Turducken Cast & Crew Bios

Adam Brooks (Jeff Holliday)
Adam is glad to be working with BPF for the first time. He is a senior at Towson University and has performed in many of their productions. His performances include "Largo Desolato", "The Crucible", and "American History SHOCKED!"
Alexander Scally (Chuck Binder)
Alexander is thrilled to be working again with Theatrical Mining Co. and Baltimore Playwrights Festival after portraying 'Nelson' in last year's Best Play winner, Gay Deceivers. A graduate of UMBC, Alexander recently returned from a brief stint in New York with Meredith's Ring, a one-act included in the Planet Connections Theatre Festivity at 440 Studios. In addition to theatre, Alexander writes and performs for Bottomless Comedy, a local sketch comedy troupe, and has appeared in lead and supporting roles in the independent feature, Bad Gig, and the online series Serious Shorts. Recent theatre credits include: Sexual Perversity in Chicago (Bernie), Interview (Interviewer #4), She Stoops To Conquer (Hardcastle), The Threepenny Opera (Tiger Brown), and The Faulkner Project: As I Lay Dying (Vernon Tull).
Andrew Peters (Director)
An actor and director, this is Andrew's first time working with Theatrical Mining Company and Baltimore Playwrights Festival. Andrew received his dual degrees in Mass Communications and Theatre Arts from Towson University this past May. Recently, he performed in a production of a dance-theatre piece at Baltimore Theatre Project entitled "Paraffin" (along with Naoko Maeshiba and KIBISM), and will also be appearing in an upcoming BPF show "Unraveled in the Gravel" as Ray. Past directing credits include "Bug" by Tracy Letts and "Antarctica" by Carolyn Raship, as well as being the artistic director of Towson University's "24 Hour Play Slam" in 2008. His past performance credits also include "The Crucible" (Putnam), "Largo Desolato" (Second Sydney), "The Diary of Anne Frank" (Mr. Dussel), "Troy Women" (Talthybius), "Urinetown" (Barrel), and as a performer in Questfest 2008's production of "Swept Away" at Baltimore Theatre Project. He has also just finished teaching at Steve Yeager's Young Filmmaker's Workshop camp for young actors as an acting coach. Outside of theatre, Andrew does graphic design work and enjoys trying to find a real job in today's thriving economic times!

She would like to dedicate her performance as Mrs. Von Shvonz to her family and friends who have always been very supportive of her "theater" endeavors…most especially to her daughter, Erin.

Joy Baldwin (Dr. Martha Grrthwkk)
Joy had her introduction to the Baltimore theatre scene with January's "London Suite" at Spotlighters and was since seen in "Lumberyard" at Mobtown. She has lived many places and finds a stage wherever she goes. Favorite roles include the dark attorney in intense "Posers" as premiered at Theatre Charlotte, the less serious Old Gobbo in "Merchant of Venice" with Risley Theatre, and the just plain crazy Old Red in the independent film, Big Bad Wolf Project. While in medical school in Vermont, she was awarded a Schweitzer Fellowship and started Prescription Laughter Theatre Company (performed comedies for the hospitalized and elderly, a spin-off of her involvement as an original member of Inspiration Theatre in Durham, NC). Equally comfortable behind the stage as on it, she also dabbled with MFA tech work at UNC to supplement her many stints in production work. In a case of life art imitating life, she also is a National Health Service Corps Scholar and works as a family physician for Jai Medical...and she promises her boss she has never made turducken on company time.

Lisa Hodsoll (playwright)
Lisa is both a writer and performer. Her historical drama, Erasmus, also received a staged reading with the Baltimore Playwrights Festival and later went on to be selected as a finalist in the Mildred and Albert Panowski Playwriting Competition. She recently had a 10 minute play (Do Us Part) featured as part of the Page to Stage Festival at the Kennedy Center. And she co-authored a screenplay, Kosmopolites, which was filmed on location in Indonesia. In Chicago, she was a member of the improv group Comedy Sportz and the sketch comedy group Brouhaha where she wrote and performed her own material. She is a member of the Baltimore based not for profit arts group, Wednesday House, and the D.C. based theatre collective Factory 449.

Turducken (metaphor)
A turducken is a dish consisting of a partially de-boned turkey stuffed with a de-boned duck, which itself is stuffed with a small de-boned chicken. The thoracic cavity of the chicken and the rest of the gaps are stuffed, sometimes with a highly seasoned breadcrumb mixture or sausage meat, although some versions have a different stuffing for each bird.
